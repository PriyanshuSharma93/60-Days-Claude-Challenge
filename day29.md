# 🎯 Objective

The objective of Day 29 was to build an interactive **Supply Chain Crisis Simulation** using Claude AI.

The project, **Operation Lifeline: Supply Chain Crisis Lab**, puts the player in the role of a business decision-maker responsible for managing a fictional company during a major supply chain crisis.

The simulator combines crisis management, supplier negotiation, leadership decision-making, and AI-powered supply chain strategy into an interactive business simulation.

The goal is to help beginners understand complex supply chain concepts through practical decision-making rather than theoretical learning.

# 2️⃣ Project Overview

**Operation Lifeline: Supply Chain Crisis Lab** is an interactive business simulation designed to teach supply chain management and crisis response through gameplay.

Every playthrough generates a different fictional company, crisis scenario, values, and outcomes.

The player must make strategic decisions while balancing:

* Cost
* Inventory
* Profit
* Delivery Speed
* Customer Satisfaction
* Supplier Trust
* Price
* Lead Time
* Business Resilience

---

# 3️⃣ Random Company Generation

At the beginning of each simulation, the application generates a fictional company with details such as:

1. Industry
2. Revenue
3. Number of Factories
4. Number of Warehouses
5. Number of Suppliers
6. Inventory Days
7. Lead Time
8. Operating Countries

The company profile is displayed using modern dashboard-style cards.

This ensures that every simulation feels different and requires a new strategic approach.

---

# 4️⃣ Supply Chain Crisis Scenarios

The simulator randomly generates one major crisis.

Possible scenarios include:

1. Factory Fire
2. Supplier Bankruptcy
3. Port Strike
4. Cyberattack
5. Flood
6. Raw Material Shortage
7. Political Conflict
8. Shipping Delay

Each crisis includes:

* Crisis Type
* Urgency Level
* Business Impact

The player must analyze the situation before making decisions.

---

# 5️⃣ War Room – Crisis Response

In the War Room, the player receives six possible crisis response actions.

The objective is to select the **three most effective actions**.

Each decision can influence important business metrics such as:

1. Cost
2. Inventory
3. Profit
4. Delivery Speed
5. Customer Satisfaction

The results are displayed using animated progress bars.

This section demonstrates how supply chain decisions often require balancing multiple competing priorities.

---

# 6️⃣ Supplier Negotiation

The negotiation phase contains four rounds of supplier discussions.

Each decision affects:

1. Supplier Trust
2. Price
3. Lead Time

The player must balance short-term cost savings with long-term supplier relationships and operational stability.

At the end of the negotiation, the simulator generates a **Negotiation Score** based on the player's decisions.

---

# 7️⃣ CEO Boardroom

The CEO Boardroom presents five multiple-choice leadership questions.

The player must make strategic decisions related to:

* Crisis Management
* Risk
* Business Continuity
* Leadership
* Customer Impact

The answers are evaluated to generate an overall **Executive Decision-Making Score**.

This section focuses on the importance of leadership and decision-making under uncertainty.

---

# 8️⃣ AI Supply Chain Strategy

The player can select two AI-powered supply chain investments.

Available options include:

1. Demand Forecasting
2. Inventory Optimization
3. Supplier Risk Monitoring
4. Warehouse Vision
5. Procurement Copilot

The simulator shows the expected business impact of the selected investments.

This demonstrates how AI can help organizations improve:

* Forecast Accuracy
* Inventory Efficiency
* Supplier Visibility
* Warehouse Operations
* Procurement Decisions

---

# 9️⃣ Final Executive Dashboard

At the end of the simulation, the player receives a comprehensive performance dashboard.

The dashboard includes:

1. Overall Crisis Score
2. Leadership Score
3. Negotiation Score
4. Resilience Score
5. Cost Control Score
6. Risk Management Score
7. Customer Satisfaction Score

The application also provides personalized feedback including:

* Biggest Mistake
* Best Decision
* Expert Recommendation
* Lessons Learned

This helps the player understand the consequences of their decisions.

# 🔟 Business Metrics

The simulation focuses on balancing several important business metrics.

### Cost

Measures the financial impact of crisis response decisions.

### Inventory

Shows how effectively the business manages available stock during disruption.

### Profit

Reflects the financial outcome of strategic decisions.

### Delivery Speed

Measures the ability to maintain customer deliveries during a crisis.

### Customer Satisfaction

Shows how supply chain decisions affect the customer experience.
