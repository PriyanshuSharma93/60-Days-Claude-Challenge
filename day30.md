# 🎯 Objective

The objective of Day 30 was to build an interactive **Supply Chain Builder Simulation** using Claude AI.

The project, **Supply Chain Builder**, is designed to help beginners understand how real-world supply chains work through an interactive decision-making experience.

Instead of simply learning supply chain concepts theoretically, users build a supply chain step-by-step by making strategic decisions about suppliers, factories, warehouses, transportation, and inventory.

Each decision dynamically affects important business metrics, allowing users to understand the real-world trade-offs involved in supply chain management.

# 2️⃣ Project Overview

**Supply Chain Builder** is an interactive educational simulation where users build a supply chain for a randomly generated company.

The application is designed specifically for beginners.

Before every decision, the simulator explains:

* What the concept means
* Why it matters
* How the decision affects the business
* What trade-offs are involved

This makes complex supply chain concepts easier to understand through practical decision-making.

---

# 3️⃣ Random Company Generation

At the beginning of every simulation, the application generates a fictional company.

The company profile includes:

1. Industry
2. Products
3. Countries Served
4. Demand Level

The randomization ensures that every playthrough presents a different business environment.

This means that supply chain strategies that work well for one company may not always be the best choice for another.

---

# 4️⃣ Supplier Strategy

The first major decision involves selecting the number of suppliers.

The player chooses between:

* Single Supplier
* Multiple Suppliers

### Single Supplier

Advantages:

* Potentially lower costs
* Easier supplier management
* Stronger supplier relationship

Risks:

* Higher dependency
* Greater disruption risk
* Limited backup options

### Multiple Suppliers

Advantages:

* Reduced dependency
* Better supply chain resilience
* Backup options during disruptions

Risks:

* Higher management complexity
* Potentially higher costs
* More supplier coordination required

This decision demonstrates the importance of balancing **cost efficiency and supply chain resilience**.

---

# 5️⃣ Factory Location

The player selects a factory location based on the company's requirements.

Factory location can influence:

* Production Cost
* Delivery Speed
* Supply Chain Risk
* Customer Satisfaction
* Sustainability

The simulation demonstrates that choosing a factory location is a strategic decision that can affect the entire supply chain.

---

# 6️⃣ Warehouse Strategy

The player chooses a warehouse strategy.

The decision affects:

* Delivery Speed
* Operating Cost
* Customer Satisfaction
* Supply Chain Flexibility

A centralized warehouse may reduce operational costs but increase delivery distances.

A distributed warehouse strategy may improve delivery speed but increase operating complexity and cost.

This section demonstrates the trade-off between **efficiency and responsiveness**.

---

# 7️⃣ Transportation Method

The player selects one transportation method:

1. Road
2. Rail
3. Sea
4. Air

Each transportation method has different advantages and disadvantages.

### Road

* Flexible
* Good for regional deliveries
* Moderate cost

### Rail

* Efficient for large volumes
* Lower emissions
* Less flexible than road

### Sea

* Cost-effective for large international shipments
* Slow delivery speed

### Air

* Fastest transportation method
* Higher cost
* Higher environmental impact

The decision demonstrates the importance of balancing **cost, speed, and sustainability**.

---

# 8️⃣ Inventory Strategy

The player chooses an inventory strategy:

1. Low Inventory
2. Balanced Inventory
3. High Inventory

### Low Inventory

Potential benefits:

* Lower holding costs
* Less capital tied up

Potential risks:

* Higher stockout risk
* Lower resilience during demand spikes

### Balanced Inventory

Provides a balance between:

* Cost
* Availability
* Risk

### High Inventory

Potential benefits:

* Better product availability
* Higher resilience against disruptions

Potential risks:

* Higher storage costs
* More capital tied up

This demonstrates the classic supply chain challenge of balancing **inventory cost and customer service**.

---

# 9️⃣ Live Business Metrics

After every decision, the simulator dynamically updates key business metrics.

The metrics include:

### Cost

Measures the overall financial impact of supply chain decisions.

### Delivery Speed

Measures how quickly products can reach customers.

### Risk

Measures exposure to supply chain disruptions.

### Customer Satisfaction

Reflects the impact of supply chain performance on customers.

### Sustainability

Measures the environmental impact of supply chain decisions.

Animated progress bars make the impact of each decision easy to understand.

# 🔟 Final Optimization Dashboard

At the end of the simulation, the application generates a complete Supply Chain Optimization Dashboard.

The dashboard includes:

1. Overall Supply Chain Score
2. Cost Performance
3. Delivery Speed
4. Risk Level
5. Customer Satisfaction
6. Sustainability Performance

The simulator also identifies:

* Key Strengths
* Key Weaknesses
* Biggest Supply Chain Risk
* Three Practical Improvements

This provides a complete summary of the player's supply chain strategy.
